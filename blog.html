About CSM Scott Bailey

Command Sergeant Major Scott Allen Bailey was a highly decorated soldier who tragically lost his life on November 4, 2017. He proudly served 30 years on active duty in the United States Army. He was laid to rest at Arlington National Cemetery on Feb 13, 2018, just days after he was scheduled to retire.

CSM Bailey dedicated his entire career to helping his soldiers. His door was always open to anybody who needed help regardless if it was a military or personal problem. He was an exceptional leader that actively engaged with his soldiers to check on their mental health and morale. Even when soldiers were no longer his responsibility, he would reach out and offer a helping hand. He was an amazing soldier, leader, mentor and father.

CSM Bailey was an inspiration to everybody he met. He was honest about his time before the military and how he decided to join the Army at the age of 25. He went from being a high school drop out to a command sergeant major in the Army. In addition to his many military achievements, he earned his bachelor’s degree in 2014 from Franklin University.

CSM Bailey entered the Army on February 22, 1989 and served until his death on Nov 4, 2017. He deployed to Iraq three times. He served as the battalion command sergeant major in Iraq from July 2008- 2009 with 6/17 Cavalry. CSM Bailey served as the brigade command sergeant major for the Warrior Transition Brigade at Fort Hood, Texas. His final duty station was at Fort Eustis where he served as the Portfolio Manager for the 128th Aviation Brigade. CSM Bailey’s military awards include: The Legion of Merit, Bronze Star Medal, Meritorious Service Medal, Joint Service Commendation Medal, Army Commendation Medal (4 oak leaf clusters), Army Achievement Medal (4 oak leaf clusters), Overseas Medal VII, Master Aviation Badge, and Combat Action Badge.
